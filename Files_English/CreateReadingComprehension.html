The Industrial Revolution, which began in Great Britain in the late 18th century, was a period of major industrialization and innovation that led to profound social and economic changes. Key inventions like the steam engine and the spinning jenny revolutionized manufacturing processes, particularly in the textile industry. This shift from agrarian economies to industrial and machine manufacturing led to rapid urbanization, as people flocked to cities in search of work. However, this growth also brought significant challenges, including poor working conditions, overcrowded cities, and social unrest. One pivotal figure during this time was James Watt, whose improvements to the steam engine were crucial. The era also saw the rise of the factory system and the establishment of a new working class.
